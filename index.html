Introduction

The age of the Milky Way galaxy we live in is approximately 13.6 billion ± 600 million years old. The Milky Way is an example of what is referred to as a barred spiral galaxy. 
Before it was a galaxy, it began as a spheroidal large cloud of gases and dark matter that underwent gravitational collapse as the dark matter drew the gases towards its centre.
This created a dense mass of high kinetic energy hydrogen atoms that undergo nuclear fusion and release huge amounts of energy.
Radiation from this caused a pressure that stabilises the core’s gravitational forces and would form a star.The formation of hundreds of billions of these stars would become the foundation of the Milky Way galaxy.
